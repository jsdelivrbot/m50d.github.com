Since watching (most of) *Umineko no Naku Koro Ni* I've refused to try to understand metaphors. Applicability is good, but I'm only interested in shows that also work on a purely literal level. Fortunately, *Yuri Kuma Arashi* is one of those.

Stripped of embellishment, the plot is simple enough for a picture book - and indeed one is used as a "show within the show". Lead Kureha loses her love to bears, encounters bears who want to eat her, but gradually rediscovers lost memories of her first love, and the shocking truth about why she forgot.
